Key Findings

FINDING 01

Iterative retrieval is essential for financial QA

A-RAG achieves 67.3% vs 3.3% naive RAG on FinanceBench â€” a +64pp gap. The multi-step agent never underperforms naive RAG on any individual question, confirming the paper's central thesis.

FINDING 02

QA ceiling at 19.3% signals index gaps

Nearly 1-in-5 FinanceBench questions hit the 15-loop ceiling. Of 84 PDFs, 23 fell back to evidence-text stubs due to download failures. Fixing corpus completeness is the single highest-leverage improvement available.

FINDING 03

Coverage vs faithfulness trade-off in summarization

For ECTSUM: A-RAG leads on coverage (3.45/5) but trails stuffing on faithfulness (3.70/5). The agent retrieves more evidence but introduces hallucination risk (H errors: 6 of 20).

FINDING 04

High retrieval recall on ECTSUM despite chunked index

A-RAG achieves 91.2% retrieval recall on ECTSUM's gold evidence set â€” the agent successfully locates the required chunks in 91% of cases, validating the hierarchical sentence index design.

FINDING 05

Keyword search drives QA; semantic search drives summarization

FinanceBench shows 63% keyword-search dominance (precision-oriented for fact lookup). ECTSUM shows 45% semantic-search share (exploration-oriented for topic coverage). The agent adapts its tool strategy to task type.

FINDING 06

FinanceBench QFS maxes loops at 15.4 on average

The query-focused summarization tasks on FinanceBench QFS hit the 22-loop ceiling with an average of 15.4 loops â€” and cost $118Â¢/item. These are multi-section synthesis tasks requiring comprehensive document coverage, justifying the high token budget.
